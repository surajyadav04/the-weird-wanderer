Why I wander

I write about travel the way others don't.

Not the five-star reviews. Not the bucket lists. Not the "must-see" monuments.

I write about the afternoon that had nothing in it but a cup of chai. The conversation with a stranger that went nowhere. The unnamed street that stays with you for years.

I believe that a place reveals itself only when you stop trying to conquer it — and that the true journey begins when you are finally lost.

The lexicon of Safar

Words that have

no translation

जुगाड़
Jugaad
Hindi

The art of innovative problem-solving with limited resources. A creative "hack" or a way of making things work when everything seems broken on the road.

यात्रा
The Weird Wanderer
Sanskrit

A journey, especially a pilgrimage. To travel not just for the destination, but for the spiritual growth and self-discovery found on the path.

सफर
Safar
Urdu

A journey or transition. It implies the act of moving from one state of being to another, where the traveler is forever changed by the distance covered.

धुन्न
Dhunn
Hindi

A single-minded obsession or a soulful melody. The rhythm of the road that settles in your heart, drowning out the noise of the ordinary world.

रूहानी
Ruhani
Urdu

Spiritual or soulful. A quality that touches the very essence of your being — a place or a moment that feels divinely inspired.

अतिथि
Atithi
Sanskrit

Literally translated as 'one without a fixed date'. A guest who arrives unannounced, treated with the reverence of a god on the journey.

The philosophy behind the journey

About

"The real voyage of discovery consists not in seeking new landscapes, but in having new eyes."

— Marcel Proust

My Journey

The Weird Wanderer began as a personal quest to document the unseen textures of India. I believe that every city has a voice, every landscape a signature, and every moment a hidden depth that only reveals itself when we slow down enough to listen.

What started as a leather-bound journal became a cinematic storytelling portal for those who refuse to travel like tourists.

My Approach

I don't follow bucket lists. I follow stories. I hire local storytellers, eat where the locals eat, and produce encounters rather than 'content'. Every trip results in a long-form dispatch, a photo essay, and a piece of my soul left behind.

I believe the best trips change how you think, not just what you've seen.

01

Slow Immersion

We stay long enough for the unfamiliar to become familiar, and familiar enough to surprise us.

02

Literary Precision

Every trip is documented with the care of a published essay. Names, dates, flavours — nothing is generic.

03

Ethical Presence

We pay fairly, credit locally, and leave places better than we found them.

04

Small Groups

Four to eight travellers. Enough for conversation, too few for a crowd.

The Digital Craft

Leading Smooth Scroll Libraries

The cinematic rhythm of this journal is powered by Lenis, a lightweight, robust smooth scroll library. By decoupling scroll rendering from native DOM events, we ensure a buttery-smooth, 60fps experience across all devices. The timeline of the stories flows like a continuous tracking shot.

Advanced Scrolling Patterns

We leverage GSAP (GreenSock Animation Platform) and ScrollTrigger to choreograph advanced parallax patterns, staggered text reveals, and atmospheric climate effects. Every scroll interaction is a deliberate storytelling choice, grounding you deeper into the narrative landscape.
